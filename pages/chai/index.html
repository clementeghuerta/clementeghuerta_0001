Steps into my process

1. Research

Analyzing Previous Website

We brainstormed many ideas of what we wanted to showcase on the website. We took images of the restaurant and did research on what other similar restaurant websites were doing in terms of their visual design. Below is a link to our compiled visual research.

Visual Research

2. Wireframe

This wireframe works well for the final design of the website. There was a problem in designing this wire frame because so many of the folds are connected to the home page. There was a different wireframe for the developers, which was simplified because I had to split up the development work.

3. Low-fidelity mock-ups

The low fidelity mock-ups were created quickly with many side notes to understand what the buttons did. These mock-ups were mainly used to create the composition.

4. High-fidelity mock-up

This high-fidelity mock-up was created to get a sense of the branding and composition and position for the different sections and call to action clickable areas.

5. Final draft and revisions/ UX feedback

Much attention was brought onto the final design of which we made a couple of versions and final revisions were done. After our prototype was made we used user-feedback, which showed us the eye-flow of the website.
We asked a couple of questions within a couple second intervals to see what information was most apparent in order to make changes to what our main visual emphasis should be directed.
